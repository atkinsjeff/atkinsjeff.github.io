***My post on mountain ecosystems for the PLOS Ecology Community


I am an ecologist with a primary focus on watershed biogeochemistry. I am interested in the interaction of vegetation and landscape position to influence biogeochemical cycles and effects of inter-annual climate variability on ecosystems. I employ a combination of remote sensing, lab, and field work in my research.

I also work with the Shenandoah Watershed Study and Virginia Trout Stream Sensitivity Study (SWAS-VTSSS), a monitoring and research program focused on understanding the biogeochemistry of mountain headwater streams in Virginia, West Virginia, and Maryland--including Shenandoah National Park and other public lands.

*Our latest paper is out in Biogeosciences: Vegetation and elevation influence the timing and magnitude of soil CO2 efflux in a humid, topographically complex watershed

You can see some of my work on GitHub, email me, and/or interact with me on Twitter
